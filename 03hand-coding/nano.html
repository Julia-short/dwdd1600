Nano Tanks

What is considered a nano tank?

A nano tank is generally defined as any aquarium under 30 gallons, though hobbyists often reserve the term for tanks under 10 gallons. These smaller setups come with limitations on what can be safely housed in them. While you can technically choose any size, most nano tanks are only suitable for fish no larger than 2 inches, and the smaller the tank, the fewer the options. Once you dip below 3 gallons, you're entering territory that's best suited for aquatic plants, shrimp, and select snail species. Despite marketing claims, no fish—not even bettas—can thrive in such cramped conditions. The only truly content resident of a tiny tank might be a Marimo moss ball.

Pros
  • Easy to clean up.
  • Great to keep in small apartments
  • Won't need as much space to keep
    • Cons
  • Limited species available.
  • Water parameters are more prone to nitrate spikes

    • Remember that whatever fish you choose to have, please take time to look up how many you can have in the tank.
