Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence (1776) include:

Thomas Jefferson
From Virginia; the principal author of the Declaration. He was a skilled writer and thinker, and later became the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams
From Massachusetts; a passionate advocate for independence and one of the key leaders in the Continental Congress. He later became the 2nd President of the U.S.
Benjamin Franklin
From Pennsylvania; a world-renowned inventor, writer, and diplomat. He helped gain French support during the Revolution and was a unifying figure among the colonies.
John Hancock
From Massachusetts; served as President of the Continental Congress. His famously large signature became a symbol of defiance against British rule.
Samuel Adams
Also from Massachusetts; a fierce critic of British policies and organizer of protests like the Boston Tea Party. He was instrumental in rallying public support for independence.

Each of these indiciduals played a signigicant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
